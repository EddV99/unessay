AI in Fiction

What we see in fiction is happening in real life!

Many AI in pictured in fiction is starting to to appear in the actual world and people are just starting to notice. An example is from the book, "Catfishing on CatNet: A Novel" by Naomi Kritzer. In that book they have a fictional character that gains the ability to think, like cats, and many other things.

We have something similar today! It doesn't have a cool name like CheShire Cat or have a conscious (yet), but it can do cool stuff like generating a text response, images, videos, voice, and the list keeps growing.

An Idea for a game

While playing the clicker game Universial Paperclips, it got me thinking of wanting to make a AI themed game. One of the major points that I wanted to make is how quickly AI has been thrown into our lives on how hard it might get to stop recognizing what was human made or generated by AI, so I wanted to make a "find-the-imposter" type game.

The game would be simple, in that all you would do is click stuff on the screen, but you would have to guess who/what is an AI from the moving characters. I envisioned many character designs, animations, and small details that would give the player a hint that they might actually be an AI character in this game world.

Drawing art, designing a world, and making the game takes a lot of time, so I've made a simple demo of what the game might look like.
