AI in Fiction

What we see in fiction is happening in real life!

AI are usually depicted as being evil, all-knowing, as part of a human (cyborg)servant, servent to humans, becoming sentient, able to mimic humans, and so much more. Some of these things sound kind of scary because it is.

If you've read the book, "Catfishing on CatNet: A Novel" by Naomi Kritzer, you would have seen an all-knowing AI chatbot, that seemed to have access to any technology it could connect to. In the Terminator series, the plot is about get chased by a cyborg sent by an AI to kill them. In the move, I, Robot, Sonny gains some kind of conscience. All these things are stuff we can somewhat connect to the new advancements in AI today.

Today, AI seems to advancing at an incredible pace! It seems like every week you hear about something new AI is being made to do.

Why is AI commonly portrayed as evil?

One of my favorite science fiction plots is when you have some kind of all-knowing, better than you in every way, evil AI that somehowsomehow the main characters have to beat.

But why is it so common? This is a hard question to give an answer too, here are some thoughts.

  1. Makes the fiction better!
    2. Often times we just want a good story and AI makes for an interesting villain

  2. Author has some personal worries of AI
    3. Maybe the author wants to tell the audience to start worrying about the current state of AI.

  3. They make money
    4. Making money is important for some authors and AI seems to be attracting audiences.

  4. They aren't always evil
    5. Sometimes they are just misunderstood, can't communicate to humans effectively, or don't know what to do with their new-found conscience.

An Idea for a game

While playing the clicker game Universal Paperclips, it got me thinking of wanting to make an AI themed game. One of the major points that I wanted to make is how quickly AI has been thrown into our lives on how hard it might get to stop recognizing what was human made or generated by AI, so I wanted to make a "find-the-imposter" type game.

The game would be simple, in that all you would do is click stuff on the screen, but you would have to guess who/what is an AI from the moving characters. I envisioned many character designs, animations, and small details that would give the player a hint that they might actually be an AI character in this game world.
